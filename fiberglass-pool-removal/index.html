Why Fiberglass Doesn't Break Like Concrete

A fiberglass pool is essentially a single massive "tub" of reinforced resin. You cannot simply "fracture" it for drainage like a concrete shell. It must be surgically sectioned and physically extracted from the ground. If left in place and filled (Partial Demo), the shell can eventually "float" or "pop" during Scottsdale's heavy monsoon seasons due to subterranean water pressure. Proper drainage management is critical during this process.

1
Hydraulic Balance: We carefully manage the water-to-backfill ratio during extraction to prevent "Cave-ins" or shifting of nearby foundations.
2
Diamond-Tip Sectioning: We cut the shell into manageable parts using dust-suppressed saws for safe removal through your yard.

FAQ

Fiberglass Removal Questions

Can I just fill in a fiberglass pool?

It is strongly discouraged in Arizona. A "Partial" fiberglass removal is prone to sinking and "The Bathtub Effect" where the shell traps water, making the yard permanently swampy. We recommend 100% extraction for fiberglass.

How is the shell disposed of?

Fiberglass is not biodegradable and cannot go into standard construction recycling. We transport the sectioned shell to certified Maricopa hazardous/industrial waste facilities for proper ecological disposal.

Will this damage my patio?

By use of "Shield & Lift" techniques, we can often extract shells while only removing the immediate 12–24 inches of surrounding deck, preserving as much of your existing patio as possible.

Is it more expensive than concrete?

Actually, often less! While the extraction logic is more complex, the material volume is lower, reducing hauling and disposal costs compared to massive concrete shells.
