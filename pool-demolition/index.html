DEMOLITION SCIENCE

The Engineering of Controlled Failure

In Scottsdale, pool demolition is often oversimplified. A 30,000-gallon concrete pool shell is a massive structural battery, holding thousands of pounds of compressive force. When you introduce a 1,500lb hydraulic breaker into that environment, you aren't just "breaking" concrete; you are managing the transfer of kinetic energy. If that energy isn't managed correctly, it can travel through the soil as a seismic wave, potentially cracking the foundations of your home, shattering your neighbor's shared masonry wall, or damaging subterranean utility conduits.

Vibration Isolation & Decoupling

Arroyo Excavation utilizes a **Vibration Decoupling** protocol. Before our heavy breakers engage the pool shell, we utilize diamond-blade saws to "relief cut" the concrete beam. This physical separation ensures that the impact energy from the breaker remains localized within the pool shell and does not transfer into the surrounding landscape. We also utilize Tier-4 muffled hydraulic hammers that operate at specific frequencies designed to break the bond of high-strength gunite while minimizing the low-frequency "ground-shake" that causes structural damage to nearby homes. PROPRIETARY PROCESS

The Arroyo "Punch-and-Strip" Decommissioning

Most contractors "smash" a hole. We engineer a drainage network. Here is why the distinction matters for your yard's future.

Vertical Wall Stripping

We don't just break the top rim. Our crews "strip" the vertical walls down to a minimum of 24–36 inches below the final grade. This ensures that when your landscaper installs irrigation lines, trees, or decorative walls, they aren't hitting concrete "ghosts" hidden just below the surface. This depth is the Scottsdale standard for true landscaping flexibility.

Multi-Point Perforation Audit

Partial demolition essentially turns your pool into a concrete "bowl." Without proper drainage, that bowl will fill with rainwater, leading to soil saturation and "pumping." We utilize high-tonnage hydraulic punches to create a minimum of 6–10 multi-directional drainage ports through the floor of the shell, ensuring that sub-surface water can return to the natural aquifer.

Steel-to-Soil Bonding Prevention

Exposed rebar is the enemy of stable fill. During our demolition, we systematically "strip" the concrete away from the structural steel, ensuring that the final backfill material bonds directly with the aggregate, not just the metal. This prevents the "void-creation" that occurs when rebar rusts and expands over decades, protecting your yard from future settlement.

Aggregate Sizing & Grading

The broken concrete isn't just "left in the hole." We manage the sizing of the debris to ensure it creates a stable, high-porosity drainage layer. By controlling the "Gradation" of the broken material, we create a sub-surface environment that supports the heavy compacted lifts above it without risk of future collapse.

HYDROLOGICAL AUTHORITY

Managing Scottsdale's Sub-Surface Drainage

A critical failure in standard pool demolition is the "Bathtub Effect." When a pool is partially demolished and filled with soil, the remaining concrete shell can act as an impermeable basin. During Scottsdale's intense monsoon seasons, rainwater can collect in this basin, Saturating the fill material and causing the soil to "pump" or liquefy. This leads to immediate settlement and can destroy any landscaping or paving installed on top.

The Arroyo Perforation Standard

To combat this, Arroyo Excavation utilizes a multi-point vertical perforation audit. We don't just punch a single hole; we engineer a network of primary and secondary drainage ports throughout the deep-end and transitions of the shell. These ports are then capped with high-porosity aggregate (gravel) to prevent soil from clogging the drainage route. This ensures that your former pool area drains at the same rate as the surrounding native desert soil, preserving the structural integrity of your yard and preventing the "swamp" conditions that plague uncertified projects. SAFETY LEADERSHIP

The Zero-Incident Safety Protocol

Demolition is a high-stakes operation. We protect your home, your neighbors, and our crew through rigorous technical oversight.

Utility Hardening

Before the first breaker hits the site, we perform a multi-point utility audit. We don't just "call 811"—we utilize private locators to map fiber-optic, irrigation, and gas lines that may cross the project area. We then "harden" these lines using physical barriers and protective plating to ensure zero-contact during the demolition phase.

Acoustic Shielding

We utilize physical acoustic barriers for projects in high-density Scottsdale neighborhoods. These sound-dampening panels reduce the decibel level of our breakers by up to 25%, maintaining the tranquility of the neighborhood and preventing HOA noise complaints during the work day.

Vibration Monitoring

On sensitive sites—such as those adjacent to historic properties or shared retaining walls—we deploy digital seismographs to monitor ground vibration in real-time. This allows our operators to adjust their impact frequency instantly, ensuring that we stay well below the threshold for structural damage.

Our safety protocol ends with a "Decontamination Phase." We utilize industrial power-sweepers to remove every grain of construction dust and debris from the community roads and your driveway.
