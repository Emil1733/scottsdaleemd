OUR MISSION

The Engineering Philosophy

Arroyo Excavation was founded on a simple, technical observation: most pool removals in Scottsdale are performed by landscaping crews who treat the project as a simple dirt-moving task. In reality, removing a 30,000-gallon structural shell from a residential lot is a complex geotechnical event that requires the precision of a structural engineer and the logistics of a master-planned builder.

The "F1 Mechanic" Approach

We often compare our crew to an F1 pit crew. In the high-stakes world of Scottsdale real estate, there is zero margin for error. A single hair-line fracture in a shared wall, a nicked high-pressure gas line, or a 1% failure in soil density can lead to tens of thousands of dollars in property damage and legal liabilities. Our crews are trained not just to operate machines, but to understand **Soil Mechanics**, **Acoustic Dampening**, and **Structural Vibration Isolation**. We don't just "fill a hole"; we restore the structural integrity of your land, ensuring that it is physically indistinguishable from the surrounding native soil. This commitment to geotechnical perfection is why the most discerning homeowners in Paradise Valley and North Scottsdale trust us with their estate properties. This trust is built on a foundation of safety, transparency, and technical mastery that ensures every project we undertake is a benchmark for the Scottsdale excavation industry. Our mission is to continue setting the standard for professional pool decommissioning, combining the technical rigor of a civil engineering firm with the personalized service of a local family business. We are proud to be the trusted partner for Scottsdale’s most challenging and prestigious residential excavation projects, proving our worth with every project we take on for the community we love and serve, for our Scottsdale neighbors and their families.

The Core Arroyo Mandate

GEOLOGICAL AUTHORITY

Fifteen Years of Desert Mastery

From the shifting clays of South Scottsdale to the solid granite peaks of Troon, we have mapped the Valley's subterranean DNA.

Managing the Caliche Barrier

In North Scottsdale, projects often stall when standard excavators hit the "Caliche" layer—a cement-like geological formation that destroys standard buckets. Arroyo owns a dedicated fleet of 1,500lb hydraulic breakers specifically designed to penetrate this layer without sending dangerous vibrations through the soil. We don't charge "extra" for caliche; we expect it, and we are equipped to manage it with surgical precision.

High-Density Logistics Experts

Neighborhoods like McCormick Ranch and Grayhawk feature complex master-planned infrastructure. Our crews are experts in navigating tight-access side yards, protecting shared community roads, and managing the strict security gate protocols of Scottsdale’s premier gated communities. We treat your neighbor’s driveway with the same respect as your own project site.

Hydrological Compliance

Scottsdale's drainage laws (NAOS and Wash Flow) are strictly enforced. A pool removal that disrupts the natural flow of desert water can lead to massive fines. We coordinate every project with City of Scottsdale hydrological engineers to ensure your lot remains "wash-compliant," preventing erosion and protecting your landscape during Arizona’s intense monsoon seasons.

Institutional Reliability

Over the last 15 years, we have built deep relationships with Maricopa County Environmental Services and the City of Scottsdale’s permit departments. We know the building codes better than any other contractor in the valley, ensuring that your decommissioning permit is closed correctly, legally, and permanently. This institutional knowledge is your safeguard against future bureaucratic hurdles, ensuring that your property remains a liquid and high-value asset in the competitive Scottsdale real estate market for decades to come.

QUALITY CONTROL

The Integrity Checklist

To ensure that every project meets our rigorous geotechnical standards, we utilize a proprietary 15-point "Structural Integrity Checklist." This document is managed by our site supervisor and serves as the final quality control gate before we issue your compaction certification.

Zero-Compromise Standards

From the initial utility disconnect to the final road sweeping, every phase of the project is verified and logged. This includes "Vibration Impact Audits" for shared walls, "Lift-by-Lift Moisture Readings," and a full "Infrastructure Clearance Check" to ensure that no subterranean utility conduits were compromised during the demolition phase. By the time we leave your property, the former pool area is not just filled—it is engineered to be the strongest part of your yard.

ECO-AUTHORITY

A Zero-Waste Demolition Model

As a local Scottsdale business, we take our environmental impact seriously. A single pool removal can generate over 150 tons of concrete and steel debris. While other contractors may look for the cheapest disposal route, Arroyo Excavation commits to a 100% recycling mandate.

Concrete Crushing

Every ton of removed concrete is transported to an approved facility where it is crushed into "ABC" aggregate for new Arizona road beds.

Steel Recovery

All rebar and structural steel is separated and recycled at Maricopa-approved metal recovery centers.
