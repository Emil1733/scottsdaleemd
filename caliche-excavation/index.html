TECHNICAL REALITY

Why Caliche Ruins
Standard Pool Removals

Caliche is a sedimentary rock-like layer common in North Scottsdale and Paradise Valley. It doesn't drain, it doesn't break easily, and it destroys standard machinery. If you don't have a plan for the rock, your pool removal becomes a disaster.

⚠️ **The Basin Effect**: If the caliche layer isn't fully penetrated, your filled-in pool becomes a subterranean "bowl" that traps water during Monsoons, leading to sudden sinkholes years later.
⚠️ **Machine Failure**: Standard skid-steers don't have the down-pressure to break caliche. They "bounce" off the surface, leading to weeks of delays and hidden surcharges.
⚠️ **Poor Compaction**: Hard rock chunks don't settle naturally. Without moisture-conditioning and heavy vibratory equipment, you'll have massive voids under your new yard. We solve this with ASTM-certified compaction.

FAQ

Technical Caliche Questions

How deep does caliche go in Scottsdale?

It varies wildly. In North Scottsdale, we frequently encounter 2-3 foot thick layers of solid calcium carbonate. Our equipment is rated to break up to 6 feet of hardened shell depth.

Will the rock breaking vibrate my house?

We use specialized "Impact Damping" techniques. By pre-cutting the concrete shell, we isolate the vibrations so your home's foundation remains safe during the heavy rock-breaking phase.

Does this cost more than a normal removal?

Yes. Caliche excavation generally adds $1,500 - $3,500 to a project due to the specialized machinery and additional labor hours required to ensure proper drainage.

Why is Rule 310 important for rock work?

Breaking caliche creates heavy silica dust. Maricopa County Rule 310 requires strict dust control to prevent local air pollution. We use continuous misting systems to stay 100% compliant.
