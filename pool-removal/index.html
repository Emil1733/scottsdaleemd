TECHNICAL EXCELLENCE

The Anatomy of a High-Precision Pool Removal

In Scottsdale’s exclusive neighborhoods, pool removal is a multi-disciplinary engineering event. We don't just demolish; we perform a surgical extraction of the entire structural footprint.

Phase 1: Material Physics & Structural Separation

The average Scottsdale swimming pool contains between 30 and 50 tons of reinforced gunite and steel. Most contractors simply break the concrete into large chunks and bury it—a process known as "abandonment." While faster, this creates a permanent underground barrier that prevents proper soil drainage and eliminates the possibility of future construction.

Gunite vs. Concrete: Understanding the Load

Gunite is pneumatically applied at high pressure, creating a much denser and more resilient structure than standard poured concrete. In our extraction process, we utilize specialized 1,500lb hydraulic breakers that vibrate at a specific frequency to separate the gunite from the #3 and #4 rebar cages. This ensures that every ounce of foreign material is removed from your property, restoring the lot to its native, build-ready state.

Phase 2: Sub-Grade Geological Restoration

Once the shell is extracted, the "bowl" of the pool must be restored. In North Scottsdale and Carefree, this bowl is often carved directly into caliche hard-pan. If you simply dump dirt into this cavity, you create a "perched water table"—a subterranean pond that will cause your new yard to become a swamp during the Monsoon season.

The Removal Spec Sheet

  • ✓ Steel Extraction: 100% removal of all #3 and #4 copper-bonded rebar.
  • ✓ Gunite Tonnage: Calculated extraction based on shell thickness (6" to 12").
  • ✓ Hydraulic Cap: Termination of all 2" PVC plumbing at the main property line.
  • ✓ Electrical Safe-Off: UL-certified abandonment of 240V pool sub-panels.
  • ✓ Debris Disposal: Certified recycling of all concrete at Maricopa County facilities.

Hydrostatic Pressure Mitigation

Scottsdale's high desert heat creates massive expansion and contraction cycles. Our team installs a primary drainage relief system at the 8-foot depth mark before backfilling begins. This ensures that groundwater pressure can never build up beneath your property, protecting your home's foundation and your neighbor's walls.

Vertical Wall Precision

For properties in McCormick Ranch or Paradise Valley with shared boundary walls, we employ "Surgical Shoring." This prevents the removal of the pool shell from undermining the structural integrity of your perimeter walls or existing home footings.

EXPERT GUIDANCE

Pool Removal Service FAQ

Can I build a patio or outdoor kitchen over the filled pool?

Yes, but only if the removal was done using the Full Extraction method with engineered backfill. We provide the 95% compaction certificate required by the City of Scottsdale for any subsequent building permits. If a partial removal was done, the area is restricted to landscaping or non-structural pavers only.

What happens to the pool equipment and heater?

All auxiliary equipment—including pumps, filters, salt cells, and heaters—is removed from the property and recycled. We safely cap the electrical sub-panel and the gas line at the main meter according to municipal code.

How do you handle the 310 Dust Permit?

As a licensed contractor in Maricopa County, we pull and manage the Rule 310 dust permit for your project. We utilize high-pressure water misters during demolition to ensure no particulate matter leaves your property line, keeping you in full compliance with local air quality regulations.

Will you damage my irrigation system?

Before we move any equipment, we locate and flag your primary irrigation lines. While pool removal involves heavy excavation, we isolate the pool's specific plumbing and electrical, ensuring your remaining landscape lighting and irrigation stay functional.
