SPECIFIC LOGISTICS

The Unique Engineering of Vinyl Pool Removal

Unlike concrete pools which are monolithic structures, vinyl pools consist of multiple independent parts: the liner, the wall panels (steel or polymer), and the substrate (usually sand or vermiculite). A professional removal must address each material separately to ensure the lot is build-ready.

Liner Remediation

Old vinyl liners trap chemicals and moisture. We extract the liner first to prevent "chemical leaching" into your clean backfill soil.

Steel Frame Recyling

If your pool has steel wall panels, we extract and haul them to Scottsdale metal recyclers, reducing the environmental impact of your demolition.

🏊

Managing the Sand Base

Most vinyl pools sit on a 2-4 inch sand or vermiculite base. While this was great for the liner, it is highly unstable for future buildings. We treat the substrate before beginning our "Managed Lift" backfill process.

  • ✓ 100% Extraction: We don't just "leave" the metal in the ground.
  • ✓ Substrate Stabilization: Scientific moisture conditioning for the sand base.
  • ✓ Rule 310 Compliance: Managing dust during the panel removal.

FAQ

Vinyl Removal Questions

Can I just pull the liner and fill it with dirt?

No. If you leave the steel or polymer walls in place, they will eventually collapse under the weight of the dirt, causing massive sinkholes in your yard. We remove all plumbing and frame elements for a permanent solution.

Is the liner hazardous?

Older vinyl liners can contain lead-based stabilizers and other additives. We treat the liner as non-standard waste and ensure it is disposed of at the correct Maricopa facility.

What happens to the plumbing?

All PVC lines are capped or removed at the source to ensure no water traps or "dead legs" remain in your yard's drainage system.

Do I need a permit for a vinyl pool?

Yes. In Scottsdale (and all of Maricopa County), any pool decommissioning over 18 inches deep requires a permit and inspection to verify the plumbing is capped and the backfill is safe.
