THE #1 RULE

Full Extraction vs.
Partial Demolition

If you plan to build **any** structural foundation over a former pool area (an ADU, Casita, Outdoor Kitchen, or even a Heavy Paver Driveway), you **cannot** use Partial Demolition. City of Scottsdale and Maricopa County codes require 100% concrete extraction and a certified backfill to issue a building permit.

Why Partial Fills Fail Construction

When concrete is left in the ground, it creates "voids" and "unmanaged density." Over time, the soil shifts around the old shell, leading to foundation cracks in your new building. A certified full extraction eliminates this risk 100%.

STRUCTURAL SCIENCE

The Geotechnical Standards for "Build-Ready" Soil

In Scottsdale construction, dirt is not just filler—it is a structural component. To build an ADU or Casita over a former pool site, the backfill must meet precise laboratory standards for density and moisture.

Understanding the "Modified Proctor" Test

When we prepare a "Build-Ready" pad, we aren't just dumping soil and running a roller over it. We adhere to the **ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor)** standard. This laboratory test determines the maximum dry density of the specific soil being used and the "Optimum Moisture Content" required to achieve it. By moisture-conditioning the soil to within 2% of this optimum range, we ensure that the soil particles interlock perfectly, eliminating the microscopic air pockets that lead to long-term settlement and foundation cracking.

Nuclear Density Verification

To verify our work, we coordinate with a 3rd-party geotechnical engineering firm that utilizes nuclear density gauges. These devices use gamma radiation to measure the precise "In-Place Density" of each 8-to-12 inch layer (lift) of soil. In Scottsdale, the building department typically requires a minimum of **95% relative compaction** for any area intended to support a structural load. We don't just aim for this number—we guarantee it through documented engineering stamps.

The "Lift" Management Protocol

Proper compaction cannot be achieved "all at once." If you fill a 10-foot deep pool hole in a single go, the bottom 5 feet will remain loose, creating a "Subterranean Sponge" that will eventually collapse under the weight of a new building. Our protocol involves "Lift Management," where we compact the site in incremental layers. Each lift is moisture-conditioned, vibrated with a high-impact sheep-foot roller, and verified before the next layer is added. This ensures a monolithic, stable block of soil from the bottom of the excavation to the final grade.

CONSTRUCTION LOGISTICS

Engineering Your New Foundation

Once the pool is removed and the ground is certified, your architect will need to decide on the foundation type for your new ADU or Casita. Because the site was once a pool, the specific structural design of the foundation is critical to its long-term integrity.

Post-Tension Slabs: The Desert Standard

In the expansive soils of Scottsdale, post-tension foundations are often preferred. These slabs utilize high-strength steel cables that are "tensioned" after the concrete is poured. This creates a monolithic, rigid platform that can bridge any minor soil variances, providing an extra layer of structural insurance for buildings placed over former pool sites.

CONSTRUCTION FAQ

Structural & Planning Questions

Can I build a swimming pool over an old swimming pool?

Technically, yes, but only if the previous pool was 100% extracted. You cannot simply "stack" a new pool inside an old one. The new pool's structural engineering depends on being supported by virgin or certified engineered soil. If you are planning a smaller, modern pool or a "Spool" (Spa/Pool), the ground must be prepped to the same 95% compaction standards as a building pad.

What is a "Surcharge" and do I need to worry about it?

In structural engineering, a surcharge is the additional weight placed on the soil by a nearby building. If your new Casita is placed close to a neighbor's wall or an existing structure, we must ensure the backfill is engineered to support that lateral pressure. This is part of our standard geotechnical site evaluation for all structural projects.

Do you handle the utility rerouting for the new build?

Yes. Most legacy pools have gas, electric, and plumbing lines that cross the proposed building site. During the extraction phase, we safe-off these utilities and, if requested, we can trench and install the new utility runs required for your guest house, saving you the cost of hiring a separate excavation crew later.

Will the ground sink after the first big rain?

No. If the ground sinks, it means the compaction failed. Because we use moisture-conditioned "Modified Proctor" standards, our backfill is actually denser than the surrounding native desert soil. We guarantee zero-settlement on all certified structural pads.

FAQ

Building & Construction Questions

Can the city tell if it was a pool before?

Yes. Building inspectors view historical satellite imagery. If they see a pool was there and you don't have a certified 95% compaction report, they will deny your foundation permit. We provide the permanent legal proof you need.

Will my new building settle?

Not with our process. By reaching 95% Modified Proctor density, we ensure the ground is as stable (or more stable) than the surrounding virgin soil. We offer a 5-year structural guarantee on all build-ready pads.

Does this cost more than "Standard" removal?

Yes. Because it requires 100% concrete extraction, more "virgin" fill material, and 3rd-party geotechnical testing, structural pads generally cost 20-30% more than landscape-ready removals.

What if I don't build for 5 years?

The certification is permanent. It stays with the property deed. If you sell the home later, the new owner can build on the pad knowing it was professionally engineered.
