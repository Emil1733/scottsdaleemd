What is "95% Compaction" actually?

When dirt is moved, it introduces air pockets (voids). If you simply dump dirt into a pool hole, gravity will eventually pull that dirt into the voids, causing **Settlement**. 95% compaction means we have mechanically removed enough air so that the soil reaches 95% of the total density it would have if it were solid rock.

The "Modified Proctor" Test

ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor) is the gold standard for high-density compaction. It involves laboratory testing of your specific yard soil to find the "Optimum Moisture Content"—the exact amount of water needed to achieve the engineered density required for building.

📊

Managed Lifts

We never dump 8 feet of dirt at once. We add soil in 8-to-12 inch "lifts," compacting each layer with industrial vibratory rollers before adding the next. This ensures density from the bottom up.

🧪

Nuclear Density Testing

A licensed 3rd-party engineering firm uses nuclear density gauges to probe the soil. This provides an instant, scientific reading of the moisture and density levels.

📜

The Engineering Stamp

Once the tests pass, you receive a formal, stamped document for your property records. This is your "Insurance Policy" against future sinking or permit denials.

Why Certification is Your Most
Valuable Project Document

Real Estate Disclosure

When you sell your house, you MUST disclose that a pool was removed. Without a compaction report, a savvy buyer's inspector will flag the area as "unstable," potentially killing the deal or costing you thousands in concessions.

Building Permit Approval

Thinking of adding a guest house or casita later? The City of Scottsdale will not issue a foundation permit on a former pool area unless you can provide a geo-certified compaction report showing 95% density.

Liability Protection

If a neighbor's wall cracks or your own patio sinks because the soil shifted, this report proves the work was done to professional engineering standards, protecting you from negligence claims.
