Removing a pool in the 85262 zip code often means contending with a shell that has been "bonded" to the underlying granite shelf. COMPACTION AUTHORITY

Managing Compaction at Elevation

At the higher elevations of Troon North, the soil composition shifts primarily to "Decomposed Granite" (DG). While DG is an excellent base for construction, it requires a specific moisture-conditioning protocol to achieve the **95% Modified Proctor** density required for structural certification.

Moisture-Conditioning & Micro-Voids

If DG is backfilled too dry, it creates "micro-voids"—tiny pockets of air between the granite particles that will eventually collapse when saturated by monsoon rains. This leading to the "settlement sinkholes" common with amateur pool removals in North Scottsdale. We utilize high-capacity water tankers and nuclear moisture gauges to ensure that every 12-inch "lift" of soil is at its **Optimum Moisture Content (OMC)** before compaction begins. This scientific approach ensures that your yard remains perfectly level and structurally sound, regardless of the intense weather patterns common in the Troon foothills.
