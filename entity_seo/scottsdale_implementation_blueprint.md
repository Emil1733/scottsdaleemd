# Scottsdale Implementation Blueprint: Pages & Sections

This document outlines the content architecture required to satisfy both Google’s Knowledge Graph (indexing) and high-ticket homeowner needs (conversion).

## 1. Current Page Audit (What We Have)
*   **Homepage (`/index.html`)**: Broad market overview.
*   **Service Landing (`/pool-removal/index.html`)**: General service descriptions.
*   **Demolition Page (`/pool-demolition/index.html`)**: Partial removal keywords.
*   **Cost Guide (`/pool-removal-cost/index.html`)**: High-intent "money" keyword target.
*   **Support Pages**: About, Contact, Blog.

## 3. Section Blueprint (Structural Requirements)

### Every New Page MUST Include:
1.  **Entity-Specific Hero**: Title must include the specific technical or location entity (e.g., "Caliche Excavation Specialists in Scottsdale").
2.  **The "Arid Problem" Section**: Educational content explaining the Scottsdale-specific challenge (soil hardness, monsoons, heat).
3.  **The "Proof of Work" Section**: Mentioning specific ROC licenses and Maricopa County standards.
4.  **The "Contextual Footer"**: A 2-column list of links to all other Entity pages on the site to lower our "Relationship Score" in Google.

---

## 4. How We Make These Pages "Worthy"

### 🛡️ Satisfying Google (Indexing Strategy)
*   **Semantic Density**: We will naturally weave in terms like "hardpan," "decomposed granite," "MCESD," and "Rule 310."
*   **Schema Markup**: Using `Service` and `LocalBusiness` schema with `areaServed` and `knowsAbout` properties.
*   **Site Topology**: Ensuring every page is internal-linked from the global footer and sitemap to avoid "Thin Content" flags.

### 💎 Satisfying Users (Conversion Strategy)
*   **Direct Answers**: Users want to know: "Will my yard sink?" and "How much does it cost?" We answer these in the first 2 scrolls.
*   **Visual Trust**: Use of the "Mini Calculator" on every sub-page to provide immediate value.
*   **Resale Value Protection**: We frame pool removal as a "Property Value Upgrade" (ready for ADU, ready for resale) rather than just a demolition job.
