Scottsdale Pool Removal Cost Guide 2026

By Arroyo Excavation Co. · 8 min read · Updated April 2026

If you're a Scottsdale homeowner researching the cost of pool removal, you've likely found a wide range of numbers online — anywhere from $5,000 to $25,000+. That range is real, and it's driven by factors specific to Maricopa County that most generic guides completely miss.

This guide gives you the real 2026 numbers, straight from active local projects.

The Two Types of Pool Removal in Arizona

Partial Demolition (Direct Fill-In): The top 18–36 inches of the pool shell are broken, drainage holes are punched through, and the cavity is filled with compacted dirt. Typical cost: $8,000 – $11,000. Best for homeowners who only want turf or desert landscaping over the site.

Full Shell Extraction: The entire concrete shell is removed from the ground. Typical cost: $12,000 – $18,000. Required if you plan to build an ADU, outdoor kitchen, garage, or any structure over the old pool footprint.

The Scottsdale-Specific Cost Drivers

1. Maricopa County Permits

The city requires a pool decommissioning permit for both partial and full removals. Budget $250 – $600 depending on pool size and project type. We manage the entire permit process on your behalf.

2. Caliche Rock Formation

This is the biggest variable most Scottsdale homeowners don't anticipate. Caliche is a hardened calcium carbonate layer found in the desert subsoil — particularly prevalent in North Scottsdale, Troon, and DC Ranch. Breaking through it requires specialized 1,500lb hydraulic hammers that most standard crews don't own. Budget an additional $1,500 – $4,000 if your site is in a known caliche zone.

3. Limited Yard Access

Homes in Old Town, Arcadia, and historic Scottsdale corridors often have side gates narrower than 40 inches — too tight for a standard excavator. We deploy micro-loaders to handle these sites without wall demolition, but expect an additional $2,000 – $4,000 for the equipment swap.

4. Pool Size

A standard 450 sq ft residential pool costs less to remove than a 900 sq ft resort-style pool. Pricing scales at roughly $50 – $80 per linear foot of pool perimeter for both material and labor.

What You Should Never Skimp On

Compaction testing. A partial fill-in without ASTM D1557-certified compaction will settle unevenly over 3–5 years, cracking any surface built above it. Proper multi-stage 12-inch lift compaction should be non-negotiable — and any contractor who skips it is creating a liability you'll discover at resale. For homeowners planning to build on the site, full pool removal with a certified backfill report is the only compliant path under Maricopa County code.
