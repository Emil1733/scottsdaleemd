Navigating the 15% Grade: Engineering for Elevation

In the McDowell Mountain foothills, pool removal is a vertical challenge. When your backyard has a 20-foot elevation drop, standard demolition protocols fail.

Equipment Anchorage & Safety Factor

Removing a pool on a steep Fountain Hills slope requires more than just a skilled operator; it requires a specialized equipment fleet. When an excavator is operating on a significant grade, its "Center of Gravity" shifts, reducing the break-force of the hydraulic hammer and increasing the risk of "Tip-Over." We utilize wide-track, high-torque machinery specifically balanced for mountainside stability. For extremely steep lots, we employ tethered anchorage systems to ensure that our heavy rock breakers remain perfectly stable while fracturing the reinforced concrete of your hillside pool shell. This is the level of safety required for the high-end estates of Firerock and Eagle Mountain.

Managing Lateral Earth Pressure

Hillside pools often act as informal "retaining walls" for the mountain slope. If you simply rip out the shell without an engineered backfill plan, you risk a catastrophic "Slope Failure" where the mountain begins to creep into the excavation void. We utilize a "Sequential Extraction & Fill" protocol. As we remove sections of the pool wall, we immediately replace them with 95% compacted engineered fill. This maintains the **Lateral Earth Pressure** required to keep the slope stable, protecting your home and your uphill neighbor's property from subterranean shifting.

The "Vanishing Edge" Decommission

Fountain Hills is famous for its infinity-edge (vanishing edge) pools. These structures are engineering marvels that utilize complex catch-basins and heavy structural reinforcing to "hang" off the side of the mountain. Removing a vanishing edge pool is a surgical task. We must carefully decommission the catch-basin and the surge tank without destabilizing the primary structural footings of your patio. Our process involves diamond-tip core drilling to relieve the tension in the reinforced concrete before we begin the primary extraction, ensuring a clean, safe removal that preserves the integrity of your remaining outdoor living space.

Seismic Mitigation: Protecting Your Structural Integrity

Fountain Hills is unique because many homes are built directly onto granite or volcanic rock shelves. In this geological environment, vibrations from heavy hydraulic breakers can travel through the rock and be felt throughout the entire home foundation. To mitigate this "Seismic Transfer," Arroyo Excavation utilizes a "Perimeter Isolation" technique. Before our heavy breakers touch the main pool shell, we use diamond-blade saw-cutting to create a physical gap between the pool deck and any adjacent home foundations or retaining walls. This "air gap" acts as a vibration dampener, ensuring that the energy from the rock excavation is contained within the pool footprint. We take every precaution to protect the structural integrity of your hillside estate, providing a surgical removal process that prioritizes your home's long-term safety.

HILLSIDE FAQ

Technical Mountainside Questions

Can a pool removal cause my hillside to shift?

If performed by an amateur, yes. The removal of a structural pool shell changes the lateral pressure on the hillside. This is why we utilize "Balanced Backfilling"—where we fill and compact the lower sections of the excavation before removing the upper sections of the shell. This maintains the structural tension of the slope, ensuring your yard remains as solid as the day the home was built. We provide the compaction logs needed to prove this stability to future buyers or building inspectors.

How do you handle the high winds in Fountain Hills?

Fountain Hills is prone to sudden, high-velocity winds that can turn construction dust into a major liability. We utilize Rule 310 compliant dust-control systems that include multi-stage misting and soil stabilizers. By saturating the fill material at the point of impact, we ensure that zero particulate matter leaves your lot, protecting your home's exterior and your neighbors' pools from construction debris.

What is a "Geotechnical Stamp" and do I need one?

In many parts of Fountain Hills, especially on steep-grade lots, the town requires a "Geotechnical Stamp" for pool decommissioning. This is a certification from a licensed engineer verifying that the backfill meets structural density requirements (typically 95% Modified Proctor). We coordinate directly with local engineering firms to provide this documentation, ensuring your project is fully compliant and legally protected.

Do you work with the Town of Fountain Hills building department?

Yes. Fountain Hills has its own specific building codes and permit requirements that differ from the City of Scottsdale. We handle the entire submittal process, from the initial site plan to the final inspection and permit closure. We ensure that your pool removal is recorded properly with the town, which is critical for future real estate transactions and property value protection.
